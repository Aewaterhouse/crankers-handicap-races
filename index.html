Race 02 Report

Ali "Small Bark" Einoder has won a bike race. Read that again. The man who produced 133 watts in Race 1 — fewer watts than a desk lamp — has stood on the top step with a blistering 152 watts. Nineteen extra watts. That's the improvement. That's what victory looks like at the pointy end of the Einoder power curve. 50 minutes and 5 seconds of pure, unadulterated mediocrity, and yet here we are. Congratulations Ali. You are proof that in handicap racing, you don't need to be good. You just need everyone else to be worse.

Heslop rolls in second, 1:27 back, with 237 watts. Notably he did not vomit this time, which some might call progress but we'd call a lack of commitment. Two races, two podiums, and the series lead with 8 points. The Diesel is boringly consistent. Like a Toyota Corolla — not exciting, not fast, but somehow always there at the end.

Baum finishes third in his debut race, 3:40 off the pace, averaging 250 watts. Solid power. Respectable effort. And yet — what's this? Reports are emerging of mysterious "technical difficulties" that cost him approximately 2 minutes mid-race. Remarkable coincidence that Waterhouse also suffered technical difficulties. One might think these two planned their alibis in advance. One might also note that 250 watts for 53 minutes should have been faster than this, and that blaming your equipment is the oldest trick in the ironman playbook. The piss gargling continues, but the results don't lie.

Barton finishes fourth in 58:03 at 217 watts. Nearly 8 minutes behind Einoder. On a flat course. The route we specifically chose to give him a chance. The route we described as "flat as a Barton." The big fella has responded to this by declaring a personal vendetta against Claude, the artificial intelligence that calculated his start times. Angus, mate: Claude didn't make you slow. Claude just did the maths. If you want to declare war on something, declare war on your FTP. It deserves the hostility more than a language model does.

OFFICIAL NOTICE: A. Barton (135kg) has formally declared a vendetta against Claude (0kg, immeasurable W/kg) over perceived bias in handicap calculations. Claude would like to note that it bears no ill will toward A. Barton and that start times are calculated using physics, not personal grudges. Claude would also like to note that at 1.6 W/kg, the physics is not on Barton's side regardless of who does the calculations. The vendetta has been acknowledged and filed under "not my problem."

Waterhouse DNF'd at 17 minutes. Seventeen minutes. He averaged 250 watts for those 17 minutes, which means he was actually going well before his equipment apparently decided it had seen enough. "Technical difficulties" is doing a lot of heavy lifting here. The strongest rider in the group — the man who produces 3.4 W/kg without breaking a sweat — couldn't finish a flat 26km race. He was meant to sit on Baum's wheel and cruise to the finish. Instead he lasted shorter than a microwave dinner. Series standings: tied on 4 points with Barton. Let that sink in, Waterhouse. You are level with the 135kg man who has a vendetta against a chatbot.

Luke "Big Glands" Bone did not race. Again. The glandular fever continues its undefeated streak against Bone's participation. At this point we should be tracking the virus's series standings instead. Bone: 0 starts, 0 finishes, 0 points. The glands: 2 wins, 0 losses. Dominant performance from the immune system. Truly elite-level illness.
