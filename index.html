Race 03 Report

Waterhouse, the sandbagging dog, has taken the win. After a humiliating DNF in Race 2 — where he lasted a shorter time than a microwave dinner — the strongest rider in the group came to Times Square with something to prove. 270 watts. 3.8 W/kg. A nine-minute, nineteen-second deficit erased like it was nothing. He carved through the field with the quiet determination of a man who'd been publicly compared to Angus Barton in the series standings.

Heslop finishes second. Again. Three races, three podiums, series leader with 11 points. The man is the human embodiment of a diesel engine — doesn't have the sexiest power curve, doesn't produce highlight-reel attacks, just turns up and finishes near the front with mechanical reliability. He and Einoder were separated by just four seconds on corrected time. Four seconds across 43 minutes. Four seconds was the margin between Heslop's podium and Einoder's heartbreak. The Diesel runs at one speed, and that speed was just enough.

Einoder takes third, missing second place by the width of a tyre. 156 watts and a dream. The man who won Race 2 with 152 watts — producing less power than a ceiling fan on low — continues to defy physics and common sense. At 64kg, he is essentially a racing greyhound who someone accidentally taught to use Zwift. The handicap system loves him. Physics loves him. His power meter remains unimpressed.

Barton finishes last among starters. Again. Five minutes behind Waterhouse, who started nine minutes after him. The big fella averaged 200 watts — down from 220W in Race 2, down from his dignity sometime around Race 1. He also suffered "technical difficulties" that cost him approximately a minute. When two riders in two consecutive races both report technical difficulties, one begins to wonder if "technical difficulties" is a euphemism for "the legs packed up." His vendetta against Claude's handicap calculations continues, though at this point the handicap is the least of his problems.

VENDETTA UPDATE: A. Barton's war against Claude continues into its second race. Claude would like to note that it predicted Barton would finish in 43:17. He finished in 47:08. Claude was being generous. The vendetta remains filed under "misplaced aggression."

Baum and Bone were both DNS for the second and third consecutive rounds respectively. Baum's technical infrastructure continues its losing battle against actually starting a race. Bone's glandular fever maintains its undefeated record. At this point, Bone's glands have a better win record than every rider in the series except Heslop. The immune system remains the most dominant force in Crankers racing.

GLANDS LEADERBOARD: L. Bone's glandular fever: 3 wins (DNS × 3), 0 losses. L. Bone: 0 starts, 0 finishes, 0 points. The glands remain uncontested series champions.

The storylines write themselves. Barton gets a 15-minute head start and six opportunities to crawl up the Clyde Kicker at an estimated 5 kph while Waterhouse and Baum start almost together, just six seconds apart, creating an instant chase group. Heslop starts three minutes ahead of them — can The Diesel hold off two riders with a combined 528 watts bearing down on him?

Bone has been allocated a start time. This is optimistic. His glands have not confirmed availability. His 215W FTP remains a number he once typed into a spreadsheet with no corroborating evidence. Claude has done its best.

Breaking news: Charlie Bone, Luke's 9-week-old son, has been registered for the series. At approximately 5kg, his W/kg is technically infinite (dividing any number of watts by a baby constitutes a mathematical grey area). He starts alongside Waterhouse, which Claude considers generous given that Charlie has produced more output in his first nine weeks of life than his father has across three rounds of racing. Charlie's estimated finish time accounts for nap windows, feeding breaks, and the strong probability that he will simply refuse to participate — a trait he has clearly inherited.

Einoder starts nearly seven minutes after Barton, which means he'll have The Fridge in his sights for most of the race. At 64kg vs 135kg, the Clyde Kicker should be the great equaliser — or at least the place where Einoder watches Barton suffer.
