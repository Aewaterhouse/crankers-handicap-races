The Race Brief

Welcome back, you magnificent underperformers. After Race 1's thrilling display of mediocrity on Casse-Pattes, we're doing it all again. This time on Beach Island Loop × 2 laps — 26.2km of pancake-flat Watopian coastline with about as much climbing as Barton does getting off the couch.

Heslop won Race 1 and promptly vomited at the finish line. That's commitment. 231 watts of pure gastric distress. The kind of effort that says "I'd rather redecorate the tarmac with my breakfast than lose to this lot." Don't expect the same charity this week — you're starting 7:30 behind Einoder and you'll have to earn it on a route with zero hills to hide on. Maybe eat lighter beforehand.

Waterhouse, the self-proclaimed strongest rider in the group, finished second. 253 watts and 3.4 W/kg and still couldn't win a four-man handicap race. This week he starts alongside Baum at the 10-minute mark, which in theory should be a cracking battle between two strong riders. In practice, Waterhouse will sit on Baum's wheel for 25km doing roughly the metabolic equivalent of a Sunday stroll, then roll past him in the final kilometre looking fresh as a daisy. We've adjusted his estimated time upward because we all know he won't go above zone 2 unless someone physically threatens him. Also, buy a heart rate strap you cheap bastard — although at the effort level you ride, it'd barely register above resting.

Barton — 135 kilograms of raw, uncut determination — hauled himself around Casse-Pattes at 1.6 W/kg and somehow finished only a minute off the win. On a flat course like Beach Island Loop, the big fella actually stands a chance. Physics says a heavy rider with 210W on flat ground isn't as slow as you'd think. We've picked this route specifically so Barton doesn't have to climb anything steeper than a speed bump.

Einoder finished last in Race 1 with 133 watts. One hundred and thirty-three. That's less than a household appliance. A toaster could have beaten you. A motivated ceiling fan could have beaten you. On the bright side, you're the lightest rider so those 133W at least move you along at 2.1 W/kg, which is... well, it's a number. You start first this week with a 10-minute buffer on Waterhouse. Use it wisely.

Luke "Big Glands" Bone enters the fray at 85kg and an alleged 215W FTP. The nickname isn't aspirational — it's medical. Bone is currently recovering from glandular fever, which makes his decision to enter a bike race either brave or profoundly stupid. No race data to judge him on yet, so we're taking his word on the FTP. If he fades after lap one, at least he has a doctor's note.

Mitch "The Piss Gargler" Baum joins us at 76kg and 270W FTP. An ironman athlete by trade, which means he can hold moderate power for approximately seven hours but couldn't win a sprint against a pensioner on a mobility scooter. No top end whatsoever. He also reportedly gargles his own urine, which we assume is some sort of recovery protocol and not just a personality trait. Starts alongside Waterhouse at the 10-minute mark. Baum will put in an honest shift on the front for 26km while Waterhouse drafts behind him contributing absolutely nothing. A fitting partnership: one man who tries too hard and one who barely tries at all.

Race rules: Two laps of Beach Island Loop, Watopia. Staggered start times as per the table above. No whinging about the handicap. If you get caught, you got caught. If you catch someone, resist the urge to sit on their wheel like a parasite — show some class and ride through. Waterhouse must wear a heart rate strap or accept a 30-second penalty for cowardice.

See you on the start line. Einoder goes first. Everyone else: set a timer, try not to cramp up while you wait, and for the love of God, Heslop, keep your breakfast down this time.
